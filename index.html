Job Tips For GEEKS: The Job Search is a step-by-step guide for software engineers and technology professionals that are seeking new employment. It was released in June 2013 as a DRM-free ebook available in several formats (180 pages in epub). The book is organized into four chapters that take the reader through their search chronologically, with some exercises designed to assist the reader.

  • Chapter I – Before the Search includes proactive steps to be taken prior to initiating search activity. Resumes, references, job search strategy options, and managing online reputation are covered. Blasting out resumes is not a strategy.

  • Chapter II – Starting the Search provides a guide to implementing your chosen search strategies, the pros and cons of the various search methods (tips to interacting with agency technical recruiters, job boards, etc.), and a unique tactic dubbed actively passive search.

  • Chapter III – During the Search covers activity monitoring and metrics, possible explanations for negative responses, phone screen and interview preparation/tactics, follow-up, and offer negotiation.

  • Chapter IV – After the Search provides guidance for when you have chosen the job you want. It includes advice on offer acceptance and rejection, resignation, counteroffers (the truth and what recruiters will tell you), and methods to leverage the contacts you made while searching.

    • About the author - Dave Fecak is the blogger behind JobTipsForGeeks.com, recruiter/consultant to many tech startups for over 16 years, and the president/founder of the Philadelphia Area Java Users' Group since 2000. Dave's writing has been regularly featured or referenced on Lifehacker, DZone, Fast Company, The Wall Street Journal Online, and several other tech and career sites, and is based on his experience managing thousands of job searches over the course of his career.
