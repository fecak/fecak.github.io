Job Tips For GEEKS: The Job Search is a step-by-step guide to a job search and written specifically for software engineers and technology professionals at all career levels. The book is organized into four chapters that take the reader through their search chronologically.

The book is divided into four chapters which represent the lifecycle of a job search, organized in chronological order.

  • Chapter I – Before the Search includes proactive steps to be taken prior to initiating search activity. Resumes, references, job search strategies, and your online reputation are covered. Blasting out resumes is not a strategy.

  • Chapter II – Starting the Search provides a guide to implementing your chosen search strategies, the pros and cons of the various search methods (tips to interacting with agency technical recruiters, job boards, etc.), and a unique tactic dubbed actively passive search.

  • Chapter III – During the Search covers activity monitoring and metrics, possible reasons for poor response, phone screen and interview preparation/tactics, follow-up, and negotiating job offers.

  • Chapter IV – After the Search provides guidance for when you have chosen the job you want. How-to guides on offer acceptance and rejection, resignation, counteroffers (the truth and what recruiters will tell you), and methods to maximize the contacts you made while searching.

This is the first in a series designed to help software engineers and technology professionals to navigate their job search. Dave's writing has been regularly featured and referenced on Lifehacker, DZone, Fast Company, The Wall Street Journal Online, and several other tech and career sites. The advice in the book is based on Dave's experience managing thousands of job searches, and was written with both junior and senior level job seekers in mind.
