Job Tips For GEEKS: The Job Search

Official book page

Dave Fecak (blogger at JobTipsForGeeks.com, recruiter to many software startups for 15 years, and founder/president of the Philadelphia Area Java Users' Group since 2000) has just released his first DRM free ebook Job Tips For GEEKS: The Job Search, which is to be the first in a series designed to help software engineers and other technology professionals to navigate their careers. You may have seen Dave's writing featured on Lifehacker or DZone. The content is based on knowledge gained through managing thousands of job searches, and the advice is applicable to junior and senior level professionals.

< The book is divided into four chapters which represent the lifecycle of a job search, organized in chronological order.

  • Chapter I – Before the Search includes proactive steps to be taken before starting any search activity. Resumes, references, job search strategies, and your online reputation are covered here. Just sending out resumes is not a strategy.

  • Chapter II – Starting the Search provides a guide to implementing your chosen search strategies, the pros and cons of the various search methods (tips to interacting with agency technical recruiters, job boards, etc.), and a unique tactic I’ve named actively passive search.

  • Chapter III – During the Search covers activity monitoring and metrics, reasons your response may be low, phone screen and interview preparation/tactics, follow-up, and negotiating job offers.

  • Chapter IV – After the Search provides guidance for after you have chosen which job you want. How to guides on accepting and rejecting offers, resigning, discussing counteroffers (and what recruiters will tell you), and ways to maximize the contacts you made while searching.
